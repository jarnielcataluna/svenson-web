FOLLICULAR UNIT TRANSPLANTATION

THE GOLD STANDARD IN HAIR TRANSPLANTATION

Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT) or Strip FUT is a method that removes a strip of hair from the donor site, generally from the back of the head, and is dissected into smaller groups to implant into the recipient sites (thinning and balding areas). It’s the first technique that was used in many procedures in the past and still being used greatly today due to its advantages of giving a high yield of up to 90% and a low graft rejection of 5%.

FOLLICULAR UNIT EXTRACTION

NO LINEAR SCAR TECHNIQUE

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) also known as the No Linear Scar Technique is a method of harvesting donor hairs using an FUE punch and then individually implanting the hair to the recipient sites (thinning and balding areas).

No incision will be made thus the reason why it was coined as the no linear scar technique. However, the area where hair has been harvested will not produce hairs anymore. It is a great option for clients who have a small thinning
